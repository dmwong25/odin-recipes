Tonkatsu Recipe

Description

Tonkatsu is a delicious Japanese dish consisting of a deep-fried, breaded pork cutlet. The pork is tenderized, seasoned, coated in flour, dipped in beaten eggs, and covered with panko breadcrumbs before being fried until crispy and golden. It is commonly served with shredded cabbage, steamed rice, and a flavorful tonkatsu sauce for a delightful blend of textures and flavors. This popular dish is a favorite among both locals and visitors, offering a satisfying and comforting culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • Pork loin or pork tenderloin (cut into thick slices or individual cutlets)
  • Salt (for seasoning the pork)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs (beaten)
  • Panko breadcrumbs (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep-frying)
  • Shredded cabbage (for a side dish)
  • Steamed rice
  • Ketchup
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Soy sauce
  • Sugar
  • Optionally, a dash of mustard (for added flavor)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pork:
    • Take pork loin or pork tenderloin and cut it into thick slices or individual cutlets.
    • Season the pork with salt on both sides for added flavor.
  2. Breading:
    • Place all-purpose flour in a shallow dish.
    • In another dish, beat the eggs until well-combined.
    • Put panko breadcrumbs in a third shallow dish.
  3. Coat the Pork Cutlets:
    • Dredge each pork cutlet in the all-purpose flour, shaking off any excess.
    • Dip the floured cutlets into the beaten eggs, making sure they are coated entirely.
    • Press the pork cutlets into the panko breadcrumbs, ensuring they are covered evenly.
  4. Frying:
    • In a deep frying pan or pot, heat vegetable oil to around 350-375°F (175-190°C).
    • Carefully add the breaded pork cutlets into the hot oil and fry until they turn golden brown and are cooked through, typically 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Draining and Resting:
    • Once the tonkatsu is cooked, remove it from the oil and place it on a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
    • Allow the tonkatsu to rest briefly before slicing.
  6. Serving:
    • Serve the tonkatsu with a side of shredded cabbage and steamed rice.
    • Optionally, accompany it with tonkatsu sauce, a savory and slightly sweet condiment typically made with ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and sometimes a dash of mustard.
